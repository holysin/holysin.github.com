IETLS WRITING 2013 11 13

Nov 13, 2013

Improvements in health, education and trade are essential for the development of poorer nations. However, the governments of richer nations should take more responsibility for helping the poorer nations in such areas.

To what extent do you agree or disagree with this opinion?

There is no doubt that poor countries should put more attention to improve their public services, such as the health, education and trade areas. And some people argue that the rich country should put more attention to help the poor countries. In my opinion, solving these problems is likely to need more than some aids given by the developed countries.

While it is undeniable that poor countries should improve their public services such as health, education and trade, richer countries’ help can be a effective strategy to solve these problems, but it can not solve the problem thorouhgly. More than that, richer countries have their own problems waiting to be solved, so waiting the richer countries giving the help hands is not reliable.

Some poor countries suffered from the nature disaster or war affairs, so thier public services are poorly developed, and their citizens have a impoverished life. As a result of the war affairs or the drought, flood disaster, the education can not be developed well. There may be a vicious circle that the citizens who received little education put little help on the development of their country, then the country cannot give better education. So other rich countries should reach their hands to help the poor countries solve such problems, in order to make a balanced developed world.

It’s not suffcient that relying on others help, however, I think that the most important is that poor countries should solve these problem by theirselves. we can see that some poor countries are still in the bad condition even though they received much help from other countries. The help is not the key of the problem, nevertheless, these countries has not utilized these help effectively, the government may use these supplies in other unnecessaries, or the it may be put into another war. So the poor countries should tackle these inner problems first, for example, solve the government’s corruption problem, or stop the fation, or utilize the international help effectively. In my view, only in this way, would the poor countries have a better development, and the international help would not be useless.

In conclusion, I think that the richer countries’ help is necessary to help the third world to get rid of the poor condition, But it is more important that poor countries should make their own strategies to handle the poor problem.
